Daytime battery charging (after 07:00) · kWh · days with solar yield > 1 kWh
Battery charging that occurs after 07:00 (i.e. from solar, not the overnight grid window) for each day with at least 1 kWh of solar yield. In export-priority mode the inverter sends solar to the grid first — the battery only charges from the overnight off-peak window, so daytime charging is near zero (blue bars). In self-use mode the battery also charges from solar during the day (green bars). The dashed vertical line marks the detected mode switch date.

Daily solar yield · red = battery depleted · — min SoC · — 15% threshold
Daily solar yield (bars, left axis) overlaid with the lowest battery SoC reached each day (blue line, right axis), measured from 07:00 after the overnight grid charge. Red bars are days where the battery dropped to ≤15% — all usable charge was consumed before the next off-peak window. When red bars cluster it's a signal to run the inverter in self-use mode so solar can top up the battery mid-day. The amber dashed line marks the 15% depletion threshold; the purple dashed line is the computed yield decision boundary.
